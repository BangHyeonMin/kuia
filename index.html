  • Our Story

    In the Hawaiian language, kuleana can be defined as accepting esponsibility with understanding and intent.

    It is our kuleana to serve our community to the best of our ability.

    At Maui Ku'ia Estate Chocolate, it is our privilege to support our local nonprofit community and implement pono practices into our business operations.

  • Laulima Cooperative Program

    Our company was founded with the intent to donate 100% of our net-profits to Maui's charities and nonprofit organizations that support our community.

    We've also developed a cooperative program called Chocolate Laulima, in which we partner with nonprofit organizations to enhance their fundraising efforts.

    We do this through hosting events and offering a collection of fundraising chocolate products that benefit these organizations.

  • Learn More Our Cacao

    "The answer came from what my family had already been doing for eight years, giving back to the Maui non-profit community."

    Through their family foundation, Makana Aloha Foundation, Gunars and his wife, JoRene, recognized that the needs in the non-profit community could not be met by current funding and wanted to do more.

    They decided to invest in building a chocolate company where 100% of net-profits would be given back to the Maui community, their home.

  • Sustainable Chocolate

    We currently grow 20 acres of cacao and have hopes to continue to grow our orchard.

    Our chocolate factory in Lahaina is 100% powered by the sun and isn't connected to our local power plant in any way.

    We do our best to source as locally as possible and utilize more eco-friendly supplies for our packaging and services.

    This is an ambitious, yet very rewarding, journey that we are on, and we are thrilled to have you as part of it.
