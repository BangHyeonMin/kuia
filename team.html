TEAM

  • A full-time Maui resident and retired biotech entrepreneur, Gunars started a cacao farm with the goal of making great chocolate.

    As the operation grew, he knew he wanted to tie it into the philanthropy that is so central to life for him and his wife JoRene.

    Plans for the farm, the factory and the company evolved and MKEC’s Chocolate Kuleana took shape right along with it, creating a remarkable and admirable business model.

    As CEO, Gunars is responsible for overall leadership, strategic vision and financial vitality of MKEC.

    Gunars Valkirs

    CEO gunars@mauichocolate.com

  • The owner and president of Cacao Services, Inc., Dan has worked in 23 different countries around the globe, providing consulting services to farmers and producers of high quality cacao.

    As MKEC’s Vice President of Chocolate Operations, Dan is responsible for managing operations at Maui Ku’ia Estate Cacao, the farm producing Maui cocoa beans for exclusive sale to MKEC.

    Dan is responsible for cacao sourcing, farm oversight, production planning, and the most delicious job of all: chocolate recipe R&D.

    Daniel O'Doherty

    VP, Farm & Factory Operations dan@mauichocolate.com

  • Born and raised on Maui, Palani Wright knows and appreciates this island from mountains to sea.

    After graduating from Lahainaluna High School, he earned an associate’s degree from University of Hawai‘i - Maui College.

    With a strong interest in forestry, he went to work for West Maui Mountains Watershed Partnership as a field crew leader.

    He has now been working for Maui Ku‘ia Estate Chocolate for four years, and he enjoys the interest and excitement of working with the MKEC team.

    According to Palani, there is still a lot to learn about this ancient crop.

    For this Maui boy, the entire process of cultivating, harvesting and fermenting cacao is an experience to be savored.

    Palani Wright

    Cacao Farmer palani@mauichocolate.farm

  • For Cacao Farm Director David McPherson, working outside with his hands in the dirt is a joy in itself.

    Then you add the great crew of four farmers that he oversees, plus the fact that their ultimate goal is to benefit the community—and eat some chocolate along the way—and David says he couldn’t ask for a more fulfilling job!

    David previously worked as a field crew leader for Maui Nui Seabird Recovery Project on Lana‘i.

    He also led a crew with the State of Hawai‘i Maui Natural Area Reserve and oversaw weed management on Lana‘i for Maui Invasive Species Committee.

    Whatever Mother Nature brings, from wind to wildfire, David and his team are well-equipped to handle it and Maui Ku‘ia Estate Cacao farm is thriving under their expert care.

    David McPherson

    Cacao Farm Director david@mauichocolate.farm

  • Dennis Elms is Maui Ku’ia Estate Chocolate Inc.’s master of all things technology.

    With his technical understanding and calm demeanor, he leads all aspects of technology to keep systems operating and employees happy.

    Dennis spent many years traveling as a computer consultant before falling in love with Maui and founding his own business in Lahaina, NexLevel Computers and Consulting.

    He later served as IT specialist at Montage and as IT manager at Honua Kai. With a degree in engineering technology and decades worth of experience in the field, he’s proud to be Technology Director and part of a new startup—one that gives back to the community he loves.

    Dennis Elms

    Factory & Technology Director dennis@mauichocolate.com

  • Charlene Ka'uhane, owner of Ka‘uhane Inc., manages public relations for Maui Ku’ia Estate Chocolate, Inc. (MKEC).

    Charlene is a multi-award-winning practitioner with expertise in media and community relations, strategic planning, partnerships, events, and more.

    In addition to MKEC, her client list includes Haleakala Ranch, Kā‘anapali Beach Resort, Maui Cattle Company, Maui County Farm Bureau and Maui Gold Pineapple Company/Hali'imaile Distilling Company, as well as pro bono services for Na Mele O Maui.

    Dedicated to her culture and community, Charlene creates important connections between MKEC and important industries, businesses and organizations.

    Charlene strives to create public relations programs that are mutually beneficial for her clients and her community.

    Charlene Ka'uhane

    Public Relations consultant charlene@mauichocolate.com
